The hardest thing in going back to school is to rip up with new life rythm, and create a new system to learn productively and more easily than year ago. ~Homework in a table~

The easiest way to clear it is to make a table. It should have 3 columns and many rows (however you need). It's really easy:

  1. In a first column of a thirst row you can write a title of a column: subjects.
  2. Second column can be called: to do.
  3. Third column: deadline.
  4. Then fill the rows like this:
    5. Subject To do Deadline ):
    MATHS ex.2,3,4/23
    a checking test Unite 1;
    (make sure you've learned it)     		exercises: tomorrow
    test: 03.09.19

    Now you only have to print it and be sure you've done your homework!

~The hierarchy of your homework~

Everyday when you come back home you should sit down for a minute and write every exercises you have to do (daily homework and earlier planned revision). Than set a hierarchy of subjects to do first the most important; you may run out of time, so it's best to start with the key ones.

It is important not to waste your time devoted to the necessary rest.

Here's a primary hierarchy of examples:

  1. A quiz for tomorrow.
  2. Homework for tm.
  3. Earlier planned revision for tests and long-term work.

    4. If you do it you will see and decide how much time you can use to rest!
